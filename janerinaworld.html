Chapter 1 — The Beginning

My journey began in May 1995, arriving in Mumbai on an early-morning Karnavati Express. The city was still waking up — quiet platforms, soft light, and the sense of something about to begin.

We had left home around 4:00 a.m., the hour when streets are empty and thoughts feel heavier than luggage. Sitting beside my father, the journey carried more than distance. It carried intention.

“Some journeys start quietly, yet leave lasting impressions.”

The train journey lasted about five hours. It was a Sunday, unhurried and gentle. We shared simple sandwiches, watched the scenery pass, and enjoyed the rhythm of the rails.

We got down at Borivali and took a cab to Andheri, to my uncle’s home. The house was empty — keys collected from the neighbors. We rested, letting the movement of the morning settle into stillness.

After lunch, we visited the Deshpande family in Prabhadevi. Conversations flowed easily — about families, work, plans, and futures. Tea and snacks were served, and we left with quiet clarity.

The next morning, we set out early again — this time to Chembur. I wore a simple brick-orange kurta with a dupatta. We walked through busy streets lined with small shops and residential buildings, finally reaching Balaka, a building named after a group of swans.

Inside, the home was bright and welcoming. The boy sat beside my father, dressed in a white and blue striped shirt, speaking with ease and intelligence. His sister, visiting from Australia, shared family stories. His mother prepared pohe and tea.

“Sometimes, clarity comes quietly — in conversation and presence.”

We returned to Andheri around noon. At 2:00 p.m., the phone rang. The Joshi family had already decided — they wished to move ahead. The decision came swiftly, unexpectedly.

We went to the STD booth to share the news with my mother. Her first question was simple: Did you like the boy? I said yes.

That evening, both families agreed. The engagement was set for the coming Thursday. What had begun as a quiet Sunday journey was now moving purposefully toward something new.
